PROPOSED AMENDMENT TO FY 2020-21 HUD ANNUAL ACTION PLAN - This item was removed
from the Consent Agenda for additional discussion at the request of Councilmember Ory.

The City of Chico is an entitlement grantee for the receipt of federal funds through two programs
administered by the Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD): Community
Development Block Grant (CDBG) and Home Investment Partnership Grant (HOME). Per the City's
Citizen Participation Plan, staff proposes to amend the 2020-21 Annual Action Plan, to allocate the
receipt of HOME Program Income funds. The Amendment recognized an increase in $222,387 of
HOME Program Income (PI) from repayment of loans. The City was proposing to use this additional
PI to support construction costs for the development of 59-unit affordable housing complex including
one manager unit at 1297 Park Avenue. A public notice was published on October 1, 2020 inviting
comments on this amendment. The Community Development Director - Planning & Housing
recommended that City Council adopt the Substantial Amendment to the FY 2020-21 Annual Action
Plan and authorize its submission to HUD. (MaryJo Alonzo, Housing Specialist)

RESOLUTION OF THE CHICO CITY COUNCIL AUTHORIZING A HOME FUND LOAN AND A
COMMUNITY DEVELOPMENT BLOCK GRANT FUND LOAN TO JAMBOREE HOUSING
CORPORATION FOR THE DEVELOPMENT OF THE PROPOSED AFFORDABLE HOUSING
PROJECT AT 1297 PARK AVENUE - This item was removed from the Consent Agenda for
additional discussion at the request of Councilmember Ory.

Jamboree Housing Corporation (Jamboree) proposed to redevelop the site of the existing Jesus
Center located at 1297 Park Avenue (APNs 005-136-009, 005-136-010 and 005-136-013) into a 58-
unit affordable apartment complex for extremely low- to low-income households including those with
special needs, and a manager's unit. The City receives federal HOME and Community Development
Block Grant (CDBG) funds to effectuate the development of affordable housing. Council previously
approved an allocation of CDBG funding for this project through the FY20 HUD Annual Action Plan
and adoption of the 2020-2021 City Budget on June 9, 2020. Additional HOME funds have since
become available and this request was to allocate these funds and additional CDBG funds to this
project. The project will serve households earning between 30-60% of area median income and will
assist the City in meeting goals to provide for extremely low- and very low-income housing, small unit
production and infill development. (Marie Demers, Housing Manager)

A motion was made by Councilmember Ory and seconded by Councilmember Huber to adopt the

FISCAL YEAR 2020-21 DEPARTMENTAL BUDGET PRESENTATIONS & DISCUSSIONS

Due to the challenges of the Covid-19 pandemic, full departmental budget presentations did not occur
during the fiscal year 2020-21 budget process. The "rollover budget" for fiscal year 2020-21 budget
was approved by the City Council on June 23, 2020 with the proviso that full presentations would
occur at the October 6, 2020 Council meeting. (Report - Mark Orme, City Manager)

During this meeting, a brief overall financial update was provided along with Departmental Directors
presentations of their respective 2020-21 budgets. Staff also addressed specific Council discussion
requests including:

Items of interest expressed by Council at the June 9 &10, 2020 Council meeting including a review
of public works projects with possible alternatives for additional projects; scheduled street and
neighborhood improvements; and the Solid Waste Franchise fee.

At the June 23, 2020 Special Council Meeting, Vice Mayor Brown asked for a conversation regarding
the impacts of the Camp Fire and rise of domestic violence and perhaps a better referral system
program which could include a city-funded grant to an outside service provider better suited for said
referrals. She would like to see what an agreement would look like, although she believed that other
agreements were approximately $150,000.

Specific new policies and practices in policing including a training overview provided by the Chief of
Police. The overview should provide a plan to include the steps to get every officer the required CIT
and de-escalation training within 2-years, including incoming officers into this program. It was noted
that a local program would be welcome. An implementation plan should also be included. It was
noted that the Use of Force policy would be reviewed by the Mayor's Ad Hoc Policing Review
Committee. It was further noted that the discussion of the membership of the current Police Advisory
Board would be left open for further discussion by the Council.

Councilmember Stone requested that training of the City’s front facing staff on diversity action related

items may need to occur. Mayor Schwab stated that should be included in the budget process as it
would need to be determined as to what kind of funding would be needed
