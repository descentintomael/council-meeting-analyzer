ADJOURNED REGULAR CHICO CITY COUNCIL MEETING — November 5, 2024
Minutes

Call to Order - Mayor Coolidge called the November 5, 2024, Regular City Council Meeting to
order at 6:00 p.m. in the Council Chamber, 421 Main Street, Chico, California.

Invocation — Lance Atkins, Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints
Pledge of Allegiance to the Flag
Roll Call

Present: Bennett, Morgan, Tandon, van Overbeek, Winslow, Reynolds, Coolidge
Absent: None

November 5, 2024, Closed Session Announcement - City Attorney John Lam announced that
Council met in Closed Session at 5:00 p.m. with staff to discuss the following items:

2.1. Labor Negotiations - Council was briefed; direction was provided; no final action was
taken.

2.2. Rosales v Chico - Council was briefed; direction was provided; no final action was taken.
2.3. Warren v. Chico - Council was briefed; direction was provided; no final action was taken.
Proclamation — Homeless Youth Awareness Month

CONSENT AGENDA

A motion was made by Vice Mayor Reynolds and seconded by Councilmember van Overbeek to
approve the Consent Agenda, as read, with Councilmember Tandon voting no on Item 2.1.

The motion carried by the following vote:

AYES: Bennett, Morgan, Tandon, van Overbeek, Winslow, Reynolds, Coolidge
NOES: None

2.1. ORDINANCE NO. 2605 - ORDINANCE OF THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF CHICO,
CALIFORNIA, AMENDING CHAPTERS 12.18 AND 12R.04 OF THE CHICO MUNICIPAL
CODE PERTAINING TO ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES AT CITY PLAZA - Final reading and
adoption by reading of title only

2.2. ORDINANCE 2606 - ORDINANCE OF THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF CHICO
AMENDING CHAPTERS 12.04, 12.18, AND 12R.04 OF THE CHICO MUNICIPAL CODE
RELATED TO CLOSING HOURS AND DRUG FREE ZONES FOR CITY PARKS,
PLAYGROUNDS AND GREENWAYS - Final reading and adoption by reading of title only

2.3. RESOLUTION 77-24 —- RESOLUTION OF THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF CHICO TO
AMEND THE CITY MASTER FEE SCHEDULE TO ESTABLISH FEES FOR SERVING OR
SELLING ALCOHOL AT EVENTS IN CITY PLAZA (AMENDMENT NO 353 - FEE
SCHEDULE 80.020)

At its 10/15/24 meeting, the City Council introduced an ordinance amending the Chico
Municipal Code to also allow the serving and sale of alcoholic beverages within the Chico City
Plaza at private events. The Council also reviewed a proposal to increase park permit fees for
events in the Plaza that serve or sell alcohol. The Council considered a resolution to amend
the City’s Master Fee Schedule to include the proposed new City Plaza fees. (Erik Gustafson,
Public Works Director Operations & Maintenance)

November 5, 2024. City Council Minutes Page/1

2.4. AMENDMENT TO CITY OF CHICO BUDGET APPENDICES B-1 AND B-3

An amendment to the City of Chico’s 2024-2025 Fiscal year Budget, Appendix B-1: Employee
Pay Schedules and B-3: Schedule of Job Title Allocations. (Billy Aldridge, Chief of Police)

2.5. SIDE LETTER AGREEMENT - CITY OF CHICO AND CHICO PUBLIC SAFETY
ASSOCIATION

A side letter agreement between the City of Chico and the Chico Public Safety Association
modified the Memorandum of Understanding for the period of July 1, 2023, to June 30, 2025.
It updated the job description and pay structure for the Police Records Supervisor position.
(Anmol Pannu, Senior Human Resources Analyst)

2.6. APPROVAL OF COUNCIL MEETING MINUTES OF 10/15/24

2.7. ITEMS REMOVED FROM THE CONSENT AGENDA

3. PUBLIC COMMENTS - Members of the public addressed the Council at this time on matters not
already listed on the agenda and within the jurisdiction's authority.

Addressing the Council were Vic Sbarbaro followed by Joni Ginno and Tennille Gates.

4. PUBLIC HEARINGS - None

5. REGULAR AGENDA
5.1. IMPLEMENTATION OF FREE HOLIDAY PARKING IN DOWNTOWN CHICO
At its meeting of 10/15/24, the Council directed staff to prepare a free parking program during the
holiday season in downtown Chico in an effort to support downtown businesses. (Report - Brendan

Ottoboni, Public Works Director -Engineering)

Councilmember van Overbeek recused himself from this item due to owning property within the area
being discussed and left the Council Chamber.

Addressing the Council was Greg Scott.

A motion was made by Vice Mayor Reynolds and seconded by Councilmember Morgan to give two
hours of free parking for all City lots in the downtown corridor from November 29, 2024 through
December 24, 2024.

The motion carried by the following vote:

AYES: Bennett, Morgan, Tandon, Winslow Reynolds, Coolidge
NOES: None
ABSTAIN: — van Overbeek
