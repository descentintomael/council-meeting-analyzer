ORDINANCE NO. 2564 - ORDINANCE OF THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF CHICO
AMENDING CHAPTER 19.74 OF TITLE 19 OF THE CHICO MUNICIPAL SIGN CODE TO EXEMPT
GOVERNMENTAL SIGNS

Adopted an ordinance amending the Sign Code to create new definitions for Governmental Signs
and Informational Sign Kiosks, exempt these sign types from the sign permit requirements, and add
review parameters in the Sign Code where none currently exist in the PQ (Public/Quasi Public
Facilities) zone. (Brendan Vieg, Community Development Director - Planning & Housing)

DETERMINATION OF PUBLIC CONVENIENCE OR NECESSITY FOR GAUTAM AND SCOTT INC.
DBA ARGUS MONSTER CRATE LOCATED AT 1692 MANGROVE AVE UNIT B, CHICO CA
95926 - APN 003-364-027

The Department of Alcoholic Beverage Control (ABC) had determined that Gautam and Scott Inc.
DBA Argus Monster Crate, a liquor store, planning to locate at 1692 Mangrove Ave. Unit B, had
applied for a Type 21 off-sale ABC license. This location is within census tract 7.0 with an undue
concentration of off-sale alcoholic beverage licenses. The number of licenses allowed in census tract
7.0 is four and there are currently six existing off-sale licenses. Pursuant to Business and Professions
Code Section 23958.4, a City Council determination that public convenience or necessity would be
served by the issuance of the Type 21 license is required. The Chief of Police recommended City
Council make a determination of Public Convenience or Necessity. (Matthew Madden, Chief of
Police)

This item was removed from the Consent Agenda by Councilmember Huber for further discussion.

A motion was made by Mayor Coolidge and seconded by Councilmember Denlay to determine that
a public convenience or necessity would be served by the issuance of the Type 21 license for the
business as outlined above.

The motion carried by the following vote:

AYES: Brown, Denlay, Morgan, Tandon, Reynolds, Coolidge
NOES: Huber

REVIEW AND APPROVAL OF RENEWAL LEASE AGREEMENT WITH E CENTER

AUTHORIZING VALIDATION PROCEEDINGS RELATED TO THE ISSUANCE OF PENSION
OBLIGATION BONDS

The City continues to explore various cost management strategies for its $146 million unfunded
accrued liability (UAL) with the California Public Employees Retirement System (CalPERS). At the
September 23, 2020 Finance Committee Workshop, the City and its Municipal Advisor (NHA
Advisors) made a presentation discussing the City's most recent actuarial report released by
CalPERS in July 2020 and provided a discussion on alternative repayment strategies, including a
restructuring of the UAL.

The City and NHA Advisors made a similar presentation at the City Council January 5, 2021 meeting
to assist in providing continued education on these topics. At the March 2, 2021 meeting, direction
was given by Council to initiate a court validation process which will be required should the City
decide to issue pension obligation bonds in the future, which future issuance would remain subject
to future City Council approval. (Report - Scott Dowell, Administrative Services Director)

rubbish, refuse and/or debris; (2) set a public hearing for 5/04/21; and (3) direct the City Clerk to mail
notices of the public hearing to all owners of affected parcels. (Report - Charlene Durkin, Code
Enforcement Officer)

FEDERAL PROGRAM FUNDING - LETTERS OF SUPPORT

Congressman Doug LaMalfa and staff recently notified City staff about a federal program request for
funding. The program included two separate categories of projects and staff has identified the
following projects under each category; 1) Member Designated Projects (MDP) (State Route 32
Widening - Phase 3, Southgate Interchange (Environmental Impact Report Phase only)); and 2)
Community Project Funding (CPF) (Broadband Infrastructure Deployment to low-income census
tract, Intelligent Transportation System (ITS), P-18 Sewer Trunkline project, New Fire Station
(northeast corner of Eaton Road and Hicks Lane), Airport Runway Reconstruction Project (13L -
31R)). (Report - Mark Orme, City Manager)

the Chamber.

A motion was made by Councilmember Morgan and seconded by Mayor Coolidge to authorize the
Mayor to sign letters of support for the projects identified above and include them in the applications
for funding through this new Federal program.
